EmpathyBC: Enhancing Empathetic Spoken Dialogue with Semantic- and Emotion-Aware Backchannel Realization

Anonymous Submission to INTERSPEECH 2026

1. Abstract

Backchannels (BCs) are crucial in natural dialogue for signaling understanding and conveying empathy, yet remain largely absent in existing empathetic dialogue systems. Prior work on backchannels typically adopts a coarse-grained classification paradigm, limiting the generation of concrete, context-appropriate expressions. We propose EmpathyBC, a semantic- and emotion-aware framework that unifies open-ended backchannel realization and empathetic response generation. By injecting representations from a pretrained self-supervised speech model into a large language model via dual-path projectors, EmpathyBC jointly models linguistic content and paralinguistic emotional cues. Guided by chain-of-thought reasoning, the model generates natural BCs seamlessly integrated into coherent responses. Experiments demonstrate significant improvements in BC prediction accuracy, as well as the perceived empathy and naturalness of the interactions.
